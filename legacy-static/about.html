Why We Exist

Climate justice cannot exist without social justice at the grassroots.

The Invisible Crisis

Global warming impacts the most vulnerable first. In rural communities, shifts in weather patterns destroy crops, dry up wells, and erode the social fabric. We exist to provide these communities with the policy knowledge they need to demand systemic change.

Data as a Shield

We believe that when communities map their own resources, they can protect them. By providing mobile surveying tools to local women, we ensure that indigenous lands and local water sources are documented and legally defended against exploitation.

A New Generation of Governance

In the last 5 years, PILAG has trained over 1,200 local leaders on climate action protocols. These individuals return to their district councils not as observers, but as architects of a sustainable, equitable future.
